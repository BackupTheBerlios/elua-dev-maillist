Hello guys!

I have been observing that we can't reuse a socket at net.accept, because
it creates a new socket every single call, but, we know that eLua's
networkl layer has some faults about opening more than 3 or 4 sockets at
the same time.

So, why not use the net.socket call to create a socket and use it at
net.accept as a parameter???

This way we could reuse a socket and use a keep-alive architecture instead
of grab a socket, close it, reopen, etc...

What you say about it?
