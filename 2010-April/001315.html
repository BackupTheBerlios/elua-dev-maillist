My recommendation is Eagle PCB.  I've used the free version for a number 
of simple PCBs for myself, and have the full version at work.    Eagle 
has a lot of on-line resources (e.g. the Sparkfun tutorials and 
libraries), plus I have friends who are quite experienced with it.

But you need to see what works for yourself.  When I get time (ha, ha!) 
I'll give KiCAD and gEDA a try.

Autorouting works fine for simple projects (e.g. two layer board with no 
critical signals) or for parts of a bigger project.  But certain signal 
types (e.g. analog, high speed buses) basically need hand routing.

AFAIK, Altium doesn't have a free version, and the commercial price tag 
is $4K.  I'm also skeptical about their overall approach (they seem very 
FPGA oriented, and want to be a single tool for everything: schematic 
capture, PCB layout, VHDL/Verilog, and software development (C/C++), 
which simply doesn't match my approach).

--Tony
