Hello all,
   Welcome to the list and to eLua Chris.
   You have (at least) two options if you don't need the eLua shell:

1) You can have a Lua program of yours running automatically upon
reset/power-on.
2) You can have eLua waking up directly in the Lua interpreter prompt.

For 1 you just need to rename your program (or the main script of a
multifile app) to "autorun.lua" and leave it together on the regular ROMFS.
eLua will look for a file with this name upon reset and load the Lua
interpreter and make it execute your program.

For 2 you just need to unselect the comment the #define BUILD_SHELL on the
platform_conf.c file of your platform/target. Without the shell, eLua wakes
up directly in the Lua interpreter prompt.

Best
Dado
