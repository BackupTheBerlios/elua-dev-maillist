> And if you want to spend some more (~$100) on an ARM Cortex-M3 kit,

> has a nice little OLED display, for which we have a bunch of games and 
> other demo apps too.
>
> http://www.luminarymicro.com/products/lm3s8962_can_ethernet_evaluation_kit.html
>
> It does not have the "stamp" form like the ET-STM32, the mbed or the 
> MOD711 and it is a bit limited on GPIO too.

> simple and good free Flashing program, it is powered by the USB cable,

> ready-to-use ethernet connector and other start facilities.
