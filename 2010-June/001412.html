If you're able to compile most anything else for the environment, Lua
should be pretty painless.

Depending on what you're looking for in functionality, you may find
some of the components added to eLua useful including some of the
patches to Lua core, the various modules we include, etc.. If you're
looking for help with any of those, feel free to ask.

eLua is mainly intended for bare metal installs with no OS underneath
and thus provides functionality for doing everything from basic
bootstrapping (standard startup code for various platforms) to
providing basic filesystem services internally.  The other components,
however, should be pretty reusable, though, and may either be broken
out from our sources or, if they're based on others' work, found from
their original providers.

Best of luck with Lua on Blackfin :-)
