Hello, Dado.

Thanks for your comments.

I believe that if their intention is to restrict usage to LMI hardware, they
should drop the "viral" statement from the license text. That statement is
not consistent with the intentions they state verbally or via email. It
could lead to misunderstandings in the future.

The "viral" reference could even be considered insulting to some open source
enthusiasts. Relating virus to open source software is inappropriate and to
me it does not make much sense.

It would be difficult to justify developing a driver library independent
from Luminary just because of that statement. Their library works well and
can be freely distributed. But they should remove that sentence from the
license and make the license text consistent with their apparent intentions.
Lawyers can find many ways to interpret a license so there should be little
room for interpretation. It would be better if they moved to BSD or another
common open source license that has been well tested. Yet that may be asking
too much.
