Well, it'll be interesting to see how it goes.  TI definitely doesn't try to
be "gcc-compliant", and I know they have some extra non-standard stuff to
take full advantage of the DSP (e.g. C6000 series can do 8 instructions per
cycle).

BTW, after the Beagle Board, TI has opened up a bit - all their assemblers
and compilers (but not the CCS IDE or DSP/BIOS RTOS) are available,  after
registration and hosted on Linux only, for free for non-commercial use.

The C6701 board isn't a bad place to start, because it can do JTAG over the
PCI bus (I don't have a good JTAG debugger).

--Tony
