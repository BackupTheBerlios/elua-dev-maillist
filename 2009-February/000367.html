I'm from London (England). I've not looked into BGA, but you are probably
right that surface mount will be the way to go, but manufacturing could be
done anywhere (China?). Probably the volumes are not high enough for BGA to
be economical. However even surface mount requires special assembly tooling
(and good eyesight!). I agree that the Stamp format is good, i.e. a 0.1"
pitch DIP (or maybe SIP) Pin header. This way, it can be used on a
breadboard or on a low-tech through-hole PCB. Just bring the IO pins
straight out and keep the SM module as simple and generic as possible and
then users can add their own interface circuitry at "human scale".
