> Your mention of TI Code Composer brings up a question. Do any of the eLua
> platforms use a compiler that is not GCC?  I tried building eLua with IAR's


It should _compile_, but it probably won't link. In this area, the main
problem of eLua right now is its dependency on Newlib. I plan to provide
eLua with its own libc/libm (adapted from Minix), but I'm afraid this will
take a bit more (and even more so for the floating point version). After we
do this, though, we _should_ be able to compile eLua on any platform that
has an ANSI C compiler, DSP or no DSP. While coding eLua, I payed special
attention to the ANSI C compatiblity part (there are currently some ugly
__attribute__ declarations in the code, but only in the TCP/IP uIP code, and
they could probably be eliminated by improving the build system).
