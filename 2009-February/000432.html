Bad idea IMHO (although often done) - USB has four wires while a
microcontroller has potentially a hundred or so I/O pins so you want the
chip near the I/O electronics rather than the PC. However having the option
for USB power is a very good idea - but over a cable. Stamp format need not
be larger than a dongle - in fact it allows you to use a mini-USB connector
which is much smaller than the full size one you have to use on a dongle.
0.1" pitch header pins are important because they allow you to use the
module on a standard solderless "breadboard" OR in a DIP IC socket on a
through-hole PCB of the sort that is within the reach of the
hobbyist/educationalist.
