With time eLua will support many dozens of boards. Using many IF's to make a
script portable (like in the current led.lua example) does not look very
elegant as the number of boards increases.

Should the language support port aliases in the GPIOs? For example, on the
EK-LM3S6965 port LED_0 could be aliased to PF_0, BTN_0 to PF_1, BTN_1 to
PE_0, etc. That could make scripts more portable without the large number of
IF statements. LEDs and buttons are two good uses for this but there are
probably others.

BTW - What is the proper way of checking whether a port exists from a script
(e.g. whether PZ_0 is available on a particular build)?
